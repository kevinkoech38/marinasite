Welcome to our Gallery

Fishing Boats

Mainly built for fishing purposes, for both salty and fresh water lakes. The immediate qualities of these boats include stability, strength, and durability to survive the fishing ventures across various kinds of waterways.

Dinghy Boats

Dinghies are popularly known as sailboats, rowboats or simply inflatables. Mainly for camping expeditions and fishing in shallow waters. It can also be used as a companion boat when the mothership cannot navigate to move the passengers to a safe area.

Deck Boats

They are provided with a stern power drive.Popularly used for recreational activities and measure about 25-25ft long. They have an open deck that allow plenty of sitting arrangement for a small group of people.

Bow rider Boats

Also known as the quibtessential family boat.Allow room for Eight people or more across its cockpit, bow cockpit, helm.Has a round-about style that allows room for wake-boards for swimming platforms, hence good for leisure.They are powered by a stern power engine.

House Boats

Also float house, which offers the luxury of living on water including recreational activities and holiday accomodation facilities. Houseboats are equipped with fine dining, entertainment, proper sleeping arrangement among others. Activities provided include: relaxed cruising, water sports and family sailing.

Center Console Boats

Features a hull with no cabin or foredeck with the helm at the centre.They are ideal for great fishing platforms, sports and they are suitable for harsh offshore waterways with plenty ocean fish. The centre console boats are equipped with baitwells, gunwale rod holders, fish lockers and outtriggers. The deck also provides a powerful insulation system for icing the fish storage.

Cabin cruiser Boats

A home on the sea.Suitable for relaxed sailing.They offer modern comforts such as air conditioning, heaters, power generators among others.They have a deep-V bottom, a secure shaft drive mechanism and rudder steering hence its suited movement is in salty waters.

Game Boats

Powered by petrol ir diesel engines.Mainly useful for game fish pursuit especially tuna. Passengers can continue their activities for a couple days or more due to the presence of sleeping berths, plumbing system and cooking galleys.

Life Boats

An important essential for every large boat. It's a small watercraft attached to a cruise or bigger vessel to carry passengers to a safe area at the face of an emergency.Lifeboats are equipped with essentials such as food, water supplies, flair gun and other emergency materials.

Sailing Yachts

A comfortable and luxurious yacht made for individuals to enjoy a real sailing experience. Usually suitable for short distances.

Mega Yachts

This is a perfect way to share and create special moments with loved ones. A mega yacht can accomodate upto 100 guests. Travelling in this vessel is equated to been in a beautiful, luxurious, boutique hotel but with a plus of enjoying the experience while surrounded by the peace and beauty of the sea and the feel of the cold breeze. They are equipped with activities such as spas, jacuzzi's, saunas, cinemas, disco rooms, swimming pools and golf courses.

Classic yachts

Classic yacht is made from elegant wood or sturdy steel and with quality craftsmanship since its construction dates back to 1920-1970. Its popular among purist charterers. Although its classy the entertainment is not modernized like the rest.

Motor Yachts

Consists of a built in swimming pool, jacuzzi, playground and sand decks. The motor yacht is a luxurious, comfortable, affordable and spacious yacht that accumilates certain individuals depending on the site.

Catamaran

A catamaran contains two hulls/pontoons making it perfect to travel through shallow waters. Hulls are made from tough fibreglass material which allows the ship to accomodate that reflects the interior of the ship. Fairly fast and comfortable to be in as research has shown most sea sick individuals tend to feel okay travelling inside.It's a lightweight, elegant, spacious and comfortable with soundproof staterooms to enjoy uninterrrupted sleep. The catamaran is normally propelled by wind power although some are engine powered.
